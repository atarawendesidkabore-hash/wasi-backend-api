# 2. THE PROBLEM: BURKINA FASO'S INVISIBLE ECONOMY

## 2.1 The Informal Economy

Burkina Faso has approximately 22 million inhabitants. Among them:

- **80%** live from agriculture, herding, or small trade
- **90%** of businesses are informal (without a tax identification number — NIF/IFU)
- **65%** of cross-border commercial exchanges are undeclared
- **70%** of transactions are conducted in cash, leaving no digital trace

These activities represent **real economic value** — billions of CFA per day — but they are invisible to the State, to banks, and to international partners.

## 2.2 The Consequences of Invisibility

### For the State
- Reduced tax base: the State cannot tax what it cannot see
- Blind planning: agricultural policies rely on data that is 2 to 5 years old
- Aid dependency: without reliable data, technical and financial partners (TFPs) impose their own surveys

### For Citizens
- No financial history → no access to bank credit
- No proof of activity → no social protection
- No visibility → feeling of abandonment by the State → vulnerability to radicalization

### For Public Contracts
- No real-time monitoring of contract execution
- Contractors delay work without consequence
- Average cost overrun on public contracts is estimated at 30% of the initial amount
- Beneficiary communities have no voice in monitoring projects that concern them

## 2.3 The Global Opportunity

The AI economy has created a new market:

| Potential Buyer | What They Seek | Estimated Annual Budget |
|----------------|---------------|----------------------|
| AI Companies (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google) | Training data on Africa | $50,000 - $500,000/year |
| Fintech (Wave, Orange Money, Ecobank) | Credit risk data | $10,000 - $100,000/year |
| Commodity Traders (Cargill, Olam) | Real-time pricing and logistics | $20,000 - $200,000/year |
| International Organizations (UN, World Bank) | Real-time economic indicators | $5,000 - $50,000/year/project |
| ECOWAS Governments | Cross-border trade data | Revenue sharing |

**These buyers currently pay for mediocre, years-old data.** Fresh, verified data produced by economic actors themselves would command a premium price.

# 3. THE SOLUTION: SOVEREIGN DATA TOKENIZATION

## 3.1 What is Data Tokenization?

Data tokenization consists of **transforming each economic activity into a structured data unit** (a "token") that can be:

1. **Collected** — via USSD on a basic phone (no smartphone needed)
2. **Verified** — by cross-referencing with other sources (citizens, businesses, sensors)
3. **Aggregated** — into anonymized indicators (no personal data is sold)
4. **Commercialized** — sold to global buyers via a secure API
5. **Compensated** — the data producer receives an immediate payment via mobile money

## 3.2 The Fundamental Principle

```
1 economic activity = 1 data token = 1 payment to the citizen

The farmer who reports the millet price at the Kaya market
  → produces a data token
    → receives 50 CFA on Orange Money
      → that data point is sold for 65 CFA to an international buyer
        → the State receives 20 CFA as a sovereign royalty
          → Burkina Faso grows wealthier from the intelligence of its own people
```

# 4. PILLAR 1: CITIZEN DATA INCOME

## 4.1 Concept

Every Burkinabè citizen carries valuable economic information in their head: the price of millet at the local market, the state of the road to the nearest town, their harvest volume, fuel availability. **This information has global market value, yet citizens are never compensated for it.**

The Citizen Data Income allows any Burkinabè to **earn a daily income** by simply declaring what they know, what they do, and what they observe.

## 4.2 How It Works

### Registration
The citizen dials **\*384\*WASI#** on any phone (even a basic 2,000 CFA handset). They provide their Orange Money/Wave number and location. Registration takes 60 seconds. **No identity document is required to begin.** Phone numbers are hashed in SHA-256 to protect privacy.

### Daily Declaration
Each morning, the citizen dials \*384\*WASI# and selects from:

| Option | Declaration Type | Payment |
|--------|-----------------|---------|
| 1 | Report a market price (millet, maize, rice, livestock...) | 50 CFA |
| 2 | Declare a harvest or agricultural work | 100 CFA |
| 3 | Report road conditions | 75 CFA |
| 4 | Declare a border crossing (with goods) | 150 CFA |
| 5 | Report fuel price | 50 CFA |
| 6 | Report rainfall | 50 CFA |
| 7 | Declare today's activity (even "nothing") | 200 CFA |

### Instant Payment
For each validated declaration, the corresponding amount is **credited immediately** to the citizen's mobile money account. No waiting, no paperwork, no middlemen.

## 4.3 Potential Earnings for a Citizen

# 5. PILLAR 2: DATA TOKENIZATION TAX CREDIT (DTTC)

## 5.1 Concept

Burkinabè businesses — from small traders at the Grand Marché to mining operators — hold valuable operational data (sales, inventory, purchases, employees, suppliers). Currently, **they have no incentive to share this data**. In fact, opacity protects them from taxation.

The DTTC **reverses this logic**: a business that shares structured data receives a tax credit proportional to the value of the data provided.

## 5.2 Tax Mechanism

### Principle
```
Tax owed by business:                    2,000,000 CFA
Tokenized data contributed (calculated): 500,000 CFA
Tax paid in cash:                        1,500,000 CFA

The business saves 500,000 CFA.
The State "loses" 500,000 CFA in tax revenue...
...but SELLS that data for 1,200,000 CFA to international buyers.
NET GAIN FOR THE STATE: +700,000 CFA
```

### Credit Schedule by Data Type

#### Operational Data (Tier A)

| Data | Frequency | Annual Token Value | Tax Credit |
|------|-----------|-------------------|------------|
| Revenue by product line | Monthly | 300,000 CFA | 300,000 CFA |
| Headcount and payroll | Quarterly | 200,000 CFA | 200,000 CFA |
| Supplier payments and origins | Monthly | 250,000 CFA | 250,000 CFA |
| Inventory levels | Weekly | 400,000 CFA | 400,000 CFA |
| Energy consumption (kWh) | Monthly | 150,000 CFA | 150,000 CFA |

**Tier A cap**: 1,300,000 CFA/year

#### Market Intelligence (Tier B)

| Data | Frequency | Annual Token Value | Tax Credit |
|------|-----------|-------------------|------------|
| Customer transaction volumes | Monthly | 200,000 CFA | 200,000 CFA |
| Input prices (raw materials) | Weekly | 300,000 CFA | 300,000 CFA |
| Transport/logistics costs | Monthly | 150,000 CFA | 150,000 CFA |
| Equipment/machinery status | Quarterly | 100,000 CFA | 100,000 CFA |

**Tier B cap**: 750,000 CFA/year

#### Strategic Data (Tier C)

| Data | Frequency | Annual Token Value | Tax Credit |
|------|-----------|-------------------|------------|
| Cross-border trade invoices | Per transaction | 500,000 CFA | 500,000 CFA |
| Mining output volumes | Monthly | 600,000 CFA | 600,000 CFA |
| Agricultural yield per hectare | Seasonal | 400,000 CFA | 400,000 CFA |
| Construction project milestones | Quarterly | 300,000 CFA | 300,000 CFA |

**Tier C cap**: 1,800,000 CFA/year

### Overall Cap
**The DTTC cannot exceed 25% of the tax owed** by the business for the fiscal year, with an absolute cap of **5,000,000 CFA per business per year**. The State always collects at least 75% of taxes in cash.

## 5.3 The Formalization Flywheel

```
Step 1: Business wants the tax credit
            ↓

            ↓
Step 3: Having a tax ID = business is now FORMAL
            ↓

            ↓
Step 5: Bank uses WASI data to score the business
            ↓
Step 6: Business gets a loan → grows → hires
            ↓
Step 7: More employees = more tax revenue + more citizen data reporters
            ↓
Step 8: Tax revenue INCREASES despite tax credits
            ↓
            Back to Step 1 — the flywheel accelerates
```

## 5.4 Projected DTTC Impact

### Scenario: 1,000 participating businesses in Year 1

```
Tax credits granted:         2,000,000,000 CFA ($3.1M)
Data points generated:       120,000 reports → 500,000 enriched points
Revenue from data sales:     3,250,000,000 CFA ($5.0M)

NET GAIN FOR THE STATE:     +1,250,000,000 CFA (+$1.9M)
```

**The State earns 62% more than it gave away in tax credits.**

## 5.5 Anti-Fraud Protections

Any false data declarations made to obtain the DTTC are subject to the penalties provided by the General Tax Code for tax fraud, **increased by 50%**. The cost of fraud exceeds the benefit of the credit — the system self-regulates.

Business data is cross-validated against citizen declarations: if a cement company claims to have sold 500 tonnes in an area, but citizen reporters from that area indicate a cement shortage, the declaration is **automatically flagged** for review.

# 6. PILLAR 3: DIGITAL FASO MEABO

## 6.1 Concept

Public contracts in Burkina Faso suffer from three chronic issues: **delays** (projects regularly exceed their deadline by 50 to 100%), **cost overruns** (frequent amendments increasing the budget by 20 to 30%), and **no citizen oversight** (beneficiary communities have no visibility into project progress).

Digital Faso Meabo solves all three by tokenizing public contracts:

1. **Payment by verified milestone** — instead of advances and negotiated invoices
2. **Citizen verification** — local residents confirm or contest progress
3. **Compensated community labor** — villagers contribute to the worksite and are paid directly via mobile money

## 6.2 Tokenized Milestone Payment

### Old System
```
Contract: Build 15 km road, Ouaga → Ziniaré
Budget: 2,500,000,000 CFA

Month 3:  Pay 30% regardless of progress
Month 6:  Pay 30% more
Month 12: Negotiate final payment → lawyers → amendments
Month 18: Road 70% complete, budget exceeded by 30%
```

### New System
```
Same contract: 15 km divided into 150 segments of 100 meters
Each segment = 1 MILESTONE TOKEN = 16,600,000 CFA

Token is released ONLY when:
  ✓ Site foreman declares: "segment complete"
  ✓ Citizen monitor confirms: "yes, the road is done here"
  ✓ GPS-timestamped photo submitted
  ✓ Automated quality check (cross-references labor/equipment reports)

  3 out of 4 confirmations → AUTOMATIC PAYMENT via Treasury

No government official needs to sign. No inspection mission.
No opportunity for corruption.
```

### Automatic Bonuses and Penalties

| Situation | Consequence |
|-----------|------------|
| Completed 4 months early | +10% bonus (250,000,000 CFA) |
| Completed 2 months early | +5% bonus (125,000,000 CFA) |
| Completed on time | Base payment |
| 1 month late | -2% per month automatic penalty |
| 6 months late | Automatic 2-year blacklist |

## 6.3 Citizen Verification

Each public contract is monitored by **citizen monitors** — residents living near the worksite who report daily:

```
Citizen dials *384*WASI#:

"Road project Ouaga-Ziniaré, km 8-9"
1. Work is progressing normally
2. Work has stopped
3. Workers are absent
4. Equipment isn't moving
5. Quality looks poor

Payment: 200 CFA per report
```

Citizen reports are cross-referenced with contractor declarations. In case of divergence, an **automatic alert** is sent to the project owner.

## 6.4 Community Labor (Faso Meabo)

In Burkinabè tradition, the community mobilizes for shared public works. Digital Faso Meabo **formalizes and compensates this tradition**:

### Payment Scale

| Task | Difficulty | Daily Token | Monthly (22 days) |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Vegetation clearing | Light | 1,500 CFA | 33,000 CFA |
| Earthwork / rock removal | Medium | 2,000 CFA | 44,000 CFA |
| Drainage ditch digging | Medium | 2,000 CFA | 44,000 CFA |
| Manual material transport | Heavy | 2,500 CFA | 55,000 CFA |
| Masonry assistance (skilled) | Skilled | 3,000 CFA | 66,000 CFA |
| Site coordination (Data Chief) | Leadership | 4,000 CFA | 88,000 CFA |

### Funding Source
In each public contract, the **unskilled labor portion** (typically 15-20% of budget) is ring-fenced for Faso Meabo. Instead of the contractor hiring workers from the capital, local villagers provide this labor and are paid directly.

```
Contract budget:                    2,500,000,000 CFA
Unskilled labor portion:              400,000,000 CFA (16%)
Paid directly to villagers:           400,000,000 CFA → stays in the local economy
```

The contractor benefits from labor **at no additional cost** (paid from the ring-fenced budget). The community earns income and has a stake in project quality. Work progresses faster.

```
Result: The young man joins the armed group.
Not out of ideology. Because 15,000 > 0.
```

## 7.4 The Early Warning System

# 10. PROPOSED LEGAL FRAMEWORK

## 10.1 Required Legal Instruments

### Decree Establishing the National Data Tokenization Program

**Article 1 — Purpose.** A National Sovereign Data Tokenization Program (NSDTP) is hereby established for the collection, verification, aggregation, and commercialization of economic data produced by citizens and businesses of Burkina Faso.

**Article 2 — Oversight.** The NSDTP is placed under the joint oversight of the Ministry of Digital Transition and the Ministry of Economy and Finance.

**Article 3 — Technical operator.** Technical operation of the NSDTP is entrusted by concession contract to a private operator selected according to the Public Procurement Code, for an initial period of five (5) years, renewable.

**Article 4 — Data sovereignty.** Data collected under the NSDTP remains the sovereign property of the State of Burkina Faso. The technical operator has the right to operate and commercialize, under State oversight.

**Article 5 — Privacy protection.** All personal data is anonymized in accordance with the personal data protection law. No individual data may be sold or transmitted to a third party.

### Finance Law Amendment: Data Tokenization Tax Credit

**Article X — Definition.** The Data Tokenization Tax Credit (DTTC) is granted to registered businesses that contribute structured economic data to the NSDTP.

**Article X+1 — Cap.** The DTTC may not exceed 25% of the tax owed by the business for the fiscal year, with an absolute cap of 5,000,000 CFA per business per year.

**Article X+2 — Conditions.** Data must be: (a) truthful and verifiable; (b) transmitted according to the established schedule; (c) compliant with the platform's technical standards; (d) validated by the automated quality control system.

**Article X+3 — Penalties.** Any false data declaration made to obtain the DTTC is subject to the penalties provided by the General Tax Code for tax fraud, increased by 50%.
