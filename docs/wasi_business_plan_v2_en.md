# 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

## The Company

WASI (West African Shipping & Economic Intelligence) is a sovereign data infrastructure and digital currency platform for West Africa. We operate at the intersection of three converging markets:

1. **Sovereign Data Commercialization** — transforming informal economic activity into tradeable data via USSD
2. **Central Bank Digital Currency (eCFA)** — the technical operator for WAEMU's digital franc CFA
3. **Cross-Border Payment Switching** — real-time clearing and settlement across 16 ECOWAS countries

WASI is the trusted technical operator that governments contract to validate, aggregate, and commercialize their national economic data — while simultaneously operating the eCFA digital currency infrastructure and ECOWAS payment interoperability layer.

## What Makes v2.0 Different

Version 1.0 was a data platform. **Version 2.0 is an economic operating system for West Africa.**

Since v1.0, we built:
- A complete **Central Bank Digital Currency (eCFA)** platform with wallet management, double-entry ledger, monetary policy tools, and AML/CFT compliance
- **Cross-border payment infrastructure** with FX engine, settlement netting, and 36 currency pairs
- An **AI intelligence agent** powered by Claude that answers questions using live WASI data
- **115 operational API endpoints** across 26 route modules (up from ~50 in v1.0)
- **45 database models** across 3 domains (up from 28)
- **3 client applications**: web dashboard, terminal CLI, and REST API

# 3. COMPANY OVERVIEW

## 3.1 Mission

To build the economic operating system of West Africa — where every citizen is a data sensor, every transaction flows through sovereign digital rails, and every data point generates value for its producer.

## 3.2 Vision

By 2030, WASI will operate the definitive data, payment, and digital currency infrastructure for ECOWAS — serving as the backbone for AI systems, central banks, financial institutions, and governments across the continent.

## 3.3 Legal Structure

- **Entity type**: Société Anonyme (SA) registered in Ouagadougou, Burkina Faso
- **Government relationship**: Private concessionaire under contract — NOT a government agency
- **Data ownership**: Data remains sovereign property of the contracting government
- **CBDC relationship**: Technical operator under BCEAO license — NOT a central bank
- **Government equity**: Each contracting government holds a non-dilutable golden share with veto power over sensitive data categories and CBDC policy changes

## 3.4 What WASI Is

| WASI Is | WASI Is Not |
|---------|------------|
| A sovereign data quality and commercialization platform | A data collection company |
| A CBDC technical infrastructure operator | A central bank or money issuer |
| A cross-border payment switching platform | A mobile money provider |
| A government technology concessionaire | A government agency |
| A B2B data API + AI agent provider | A consumer-facing fintech app |
| A neutral market maker for data and payments | An NGO or aid organization |

# 4. THE MARKET OPPORTUNITY

## 4.1 Three Converging Markets

### Market 1: African Data ($500M TAM)
- 400 million West Africans generating unmeasured economic activity
- 35-40% of GDP is informal and invisible
- AI companies pay $100K-$500K/year for structured African data
- Zero real-time economic data feeds exist for the region

### Market 2: ECOWAS Digital Payments ($180B+ mobile money volume)
- 250+ million registered mobile money accounts
- Cross-border fees: 3-8% (vs WASI target: 0.35%)
- $32.7B in annual remittances to ECOWAS
- PAPSS (Pan-African Payment Settlement System) has only 6 countries integrated

### Market 3: Central Bank Digital Currency ($45-55T CFA WAEMU money supply)
- BCEAO actively exploring digital CFA
- 8 WAEMU countries sharing a single currency (XOF)
- 70% of transactions are cash — no digital trace
- eNaira (Nigeria) launched but struggling with adoption
- No CBDC infrastructure exists for the CFA zone

| Market | TAM | SAM | SOM (Year 5) |

| WAEMU CBDC Operations | 8 countries × 45T CFA | $50M in tx fees | $15M in tx fees |
| **Combined** | | | **$87M/yr** |

## 4.3 Why Now

1. **BCEAO is ready for eCFA** — the central bank has published its digital currency framework and is seeking technical partners
2. **AI agents are autonomous data consumers** — MCP and tool-use mean AI can discover and purchase data programmatically
3. **Mobile money penetration is at critical mass** — 60%+ of adults in key markets
4. **PAPSS has proven demand** but moves slowly — WASI moves 10x faster with existing infrastructure
5. **Security situation creates urgency** — Sahel governments need economic intelligence and citizen engagement NOW
6. **No competitor has the three-layer stack** — data + payments + CBDC in one platform

## 5.4 eCFA CBDC Platform (NEW — 41 endpoints)

A complete Central Bank Digital Currency infrastructure for the WAEMU zone (8 countries sharing the CFA franc):

### Wallet Management
- 5 wallet types: Central Bank, Commercial Bank, Agent, Merchant, Retail
- 4 KYC tiers with progressive daily/balance limits
- PIN-secured transactions with wallet freeze/unfreeze

### Double-Entry Ledger
- Immutable SHA-256 hash chain (blockchain-grade audit trail without the overhead)
- Every transaction creates debit + credit entries
- Real-time balance computation from ledger

### Transaction Types (11)
- MINT / BURN (central bank only)
- P2P transfer, Merchant payment
- Cash-in / Cash-out (via agent network)
- Government disbursement (direct benefit transfer)
- Tax payment, Cross-border settlement

### Monetary Policy Tools
- 3 BCEAO policy rates: key rate, lending facility, deposit facility
- Reserve requirements with remuneration
- Standing lending/deposit facilities with interest
- Daily interest application
- M0/M1/M2 money supply tracking per country
- Monetary policy decision recording and history
- Eligible collateral registry

### AML/CFT Compliance
- 7 alert types: velocity, structuring, blacklist, high-value, dormant, cross-border, jurisdiction risk
- Automated sweep across all transactions
- Alert dashboard with resolution workflow
- Full audit log

### Programmable Money
- Conditional spending policies (geographic, merchant category, time-based)
- Expiry dates (use-it-or-lose-it stimulus)
- Demurrage (negative interest to encourage spending)

### Settlement
- Bilateral netting for domestic transactions
- Multi-country cross-border settlement
- COBOL-compatible output for legacy banking systems

**Why this matters for WASI**: eCFA gives every citizen a digital wallet — which is also the data collection channel. Pay citizens for their data directly in eCFA. No MNO intermediary needed.

# 7. GOVERNMENT CONTRACT STRUCTURE

## 7.1 Dual Concession Model

WASI now operates under **two parallel concession types**:

### Data Concession (per country)
- 5-year renewable term
- Revenue split: Government 30%, WASI 20%, Contributors 40%, Data Chiefs 10%

- Granted by BCEAO (central bank for 8 WAEMU countries)
